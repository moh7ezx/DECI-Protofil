My DECI Learnings

Topics I enjoyed the most

The topics I enjoyed the most during the DECI program were those related to problem-solving and creativity. I found it exciting to use tools like Blockly and Thunkable to build small applications that could respond to user input. It felt like I was bringing ideas to life. I also enjoyed working on collaborative projects, especially when we had to combine coding, design, and logical thinking together. The process of creating a portfolio was rewarding because it allowed me to see how much progress I had made. It gave me confidence in my ability to use technology to express my thoughts and creativity.

Topics I want to learn more about

I want to learn more about cybersecurity because it is one of the most important fields in the modern digital world. Knowing how to protect data and systems is not just useful for companies, but also for individuals. I am also very curious about data science. The idea of analyzing large amounts of information and finding hidden patterns fascinates me. I believe that exploring data science will give me opportunities to solve real-world problems in smart and innovative ways. Another topic I want to explore more is artificial intelligence and how it can change the future of computing.

Topics I thought were difficult

The topics I thought were difficult included understanding binary numbers and how computers actually represent information at the lowest level. At first, it was challenging to think in terms of 0s and 1s, but with practice, I started to see how important this foundation is. I also found some programming concepts tricky, like loops and conditionals, especially when combining them together to solve problems. However, these difficulties motivated me to keep practicing, and eventually I could see my skills improving. Struggling with these concepts showed me that learning is not always easy, but it is always rewarding.
